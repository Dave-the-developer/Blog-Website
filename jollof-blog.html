Jollof rice is a popular West African dish that is enjoyed by many. While there are many variations of the recipe, here is a basic recipe for making jollof rice:

Ingredients:

2 cups of long-grain rice
3 large ripe tomatoes
2 medium-sized onions
2 scotch bonnet peppers (or to taste)
2 red bell peppers
1 teaspoon of thyme
2 teaspoons of curry powder
1 teaspoon of smoked paprika
2 Knorr chicken bouillon cubes (or any other bouillon cubes of your choice)
1 tablespoon of tomato paste
1/2 cup of vegetable oil
Salt (to taste)
3 cups of water

Instructions:

Rinse the rice in cold water and set aside.
Blend the tomatoes, onions, and peppers together until you get a smooth puree.
Heat up the vegetable oil in a large pot on medium heat.
Add the tomato paste and fry for 2-3 minutes until the paste has turned a darker shade of red.
Add the blended tomato mixture to the pot and stir.
Add the thyme, curry powder, smoked paprika, and bouillon cubes, and mix everything together.
Let the tomato mixture cook for 15-20 minutes until the sauce has reduced and thickened.
Add the rice to the pot and stir until it is coated with the tomato mixture.
Add the water and stir.
Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the rice cook for 20-25 minutes or until the water has been absorbed and the rice is tender.
Fluff the rice with a fork and serve hot.
Enjoy your delicious jollof rice!
